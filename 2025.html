As I sit down to pen my thoughts in writing on the New Year day of 2025, I cannot think of a better idea for an essay besides the number 2025 that is this year.

I received this as a forward on WhatsApp:

Some interesting mathematical facts of the year, that we are approaching, 2025:

  1. 2025, itself is a square.
  2. It's a product of two squares, Viz. 9² x 5² = 2025.
  3. It is the sum of 3-squares, viz. 40²+ 20²+5²= 2025.
  4. It's the first square after 1936.
  5. It's the sum of cubes, of all the single digits, from 1 to 9, viz.1³+2³+......9³= 2025.

These are my thoughts on each of these facts of the year:

  1. 2025, itself is a square.

    Not only is this true (and interesting), may I remind it is the raison d'être for this essay? By the way, its square-root is 45.

  2. It's a product of two squares, Viz. 9² x 5² = 2025.

    Well, 45 is a composite number i.e., a product of its factors. Of course, the square of a composite number would be the product of the squares of its factors. One could have just as well provided an additional example viz., 3² x 15² = 2025.

  3. It is the sum of 3-squares, viz. 40²+ 20²+5²= 2025.

    This is interesting, but not because 2025 is a sum of three squares. Legendre's three-square theorem shows that there are infinitely many numbers that can be expressed as the sum of three squares. For example, 2024 = 8² + 14² + 42² = 10² + 18² + 40². The reason it is interesting as presented here is the choice of numbers - two of the three squares are 40² and 5².

    We know from elementary school that (a + b)² = (a² + 2ab + b²).

    Every two-digit number of the form MN is essentially M0 + N (i.e., M x 10 + N). For example, 37 = 30 + 7 and 45 = 40 + 5.

    When you expand 37² as (30 + 7)², you get 30² + 2 x 30 x 7 + 7², but the middle term viz., 2 x 30 x 7 is not a perfect square.

    However, when 45², written as (40 + 5)² is expanded to (40² + 2 x 40 x 5 + 5²), it turns out that 2 x 40 x 5 is also a perfect square!

    The other two-digit numbers that can make the same claim as 45 are 15, 51, 54, 59 and 95. In other words, besides 45², only 15², 51², 54², 59² and 95² can be written as the sum of three squares, two of which are from the digits that compose them. The careful observer would note that the digit that pairs with 5 to make the two-digit number viz., 1, 4, and 9 are the only (non-zero) single-digit squares.

  4. It's the first square after 1936.

    This one caught my attention and I took a minute to absorb it. While it's not a big deal to know that 44² = 1936, it didn't occur to me to see 1936 as a calendar-year. I am blessed to know a handful of people who have lived to see both these years (we should probably call them The Pythagorean Clique <smiles>).

    Subsequently, I received another WhatsApp forward that highlighted how 1936 and 2025 are the only squares in their respective centuries. I wonder how many of us old enough to read this, are likely to live to see the year 2116?

  5. It's the sum of cubes, of all the single digits, from 1 to 9, viz.1³+2³+......9³= 2025.

    Also, the square-root of the year viz., 45 is the sum of all the single-digit numbers i.e., 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6 + 7 + 8 + 9 = 45.

    In general, I find it remarkable that the sum of the individual cubes of the first N numbers is the same as the square of the collective sum of the first N numbers i.e., Σ i = 1 n i 3 = ( Σ i = 1 n i ) 2 . There is a just-released beautiful video that provides a geometric explanation for this. Incidentally, in that video, there are other cool facts about 2025, many of which overlap with this essay.

    To put this is a less math-y way (or in a bombastic management-y way):

    • Say you recruit a bunch of rookies.
    • You work towards bolstering the strength of each individual - to an extent that each of their strengths is now a cube of what it was (e.g., 3 --> 27, 7 --> 343, 11 --> 1331, etc.).
    • Only after this, do you put them together to form a team. Let's call them Team-P.
    • Now suppose you find a different set of individuals, but in terms of strengths, each individual here is identical to the corresponding rookie of Team-P at the time of recruitment. We will call this new team, Team-Q.
    • What is different about Team-Q is that we form a team composed of these individuals right at the very beginning.
    • Now we work on building the strength of Team-Q - to an extent that their strengths are now just a square of the original team-strength.
    • Surprise - Team-P and Team-Q will have equal strength!

I have a few more of my own observations of 2025 that I would like to share:

  • 2025 is one of those somewhat rare numbers that you can write like so: 2025 = (20 + 25)².

  • You can get to 2025 in a scientific calculator without pressing any number in the calculator. Here is one sequence of steps (I believe it is the one with the fewest steps, but I will be delighted to know if there is a shorter sequence) to get to 2025 without pressing a single number key in your scientific calculator:

    1. Press the n! (factorial) button. Since you start with 0 and 0! = 1, you will now have 1.
    2. Do a Tan-1. If you are in "DEG" (degrees) mode, you will have 45.
    3. Square the number.
